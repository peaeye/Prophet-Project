Gerrit W. Gong

Gerrit W. Gong is currently the 14th Senior Apostle in the Quorum of the Twelve Apostles. He was born on December 23, 1953 in Redwood City, CA. His parents are both converts; his mother was converted first, and his father was converted when he married Gerrit's mother. In high school, he played soccer, participated in the chess club, and hiked frequently. After high school, he went on a mission to Taiwan.

He got his bachelor's in Asian and university studies at BYU. He then went on to get his Master's in Philosophy and a doctorate in international relations at Oxford University. Professionally, he did a lot. He was a professor at Georgetown and John Hopkins University, served as a special assistant to the undersecretary of state, was a special assistant to the US ambassador to China, and served in several positions at the Center for Strategic and International Studies in Washington, D.C. He also was an assitant to the president for planning and assessment at BYU before he became part of the Seventy.

After he returned from his mission, he giving Sunday evening firesides at the Provo MTC when he met an outgoing missionary, Sister Susan Lindsay. She went off to Taiwan for her mission and he went to Oxford. During a summer break, he returned to Provo to spend time with his family when he met her again. They started dating and on January 2, 1980, they were married. They have 4 children together.

On March 31, 2018, he was set apart as a member of the Quorum of the Twelve Apostles.
